# ADR-006: Rollback Strategy

## Status
Approved

## Context
When cutover occurs, if PostgreSQL encounters unforeseen runtime locks, performance regressions, or connection pool depletion under extreme peak loads, the platform must be able to fail back to Firestore immediately without losing newly written data or causing user-facing downtime.

## Decision
Establish a structured rollback protocol. During Sprint 2A & 2A.5:
1. **Standby Mirroring:** Firestore serves as the primary system, and PostgreSQL runs in parallel.
2. **Post-Cutover Fallback:** Even after the primary reads/writes are shifted to Postgres (in Sprint 3), dual-writing to Firestore will remain active for a predetermined observation period.
3. **Execution:** If a stop condition is triggered, change `DATABASE_PROVIDER` back to `FIRESTORE` (or `DUAL_WRITE`) via environment configuration.

## Alternatives Considered
* **Database Snapshot Restoration:** Restoring Postgres from backup snapshots. Rejected as this causes active data loss of transactions written between the snapshot time and the crash.

## Consequences
* **Immediate Recovery:** Rollback takes < 5 minutes (time to update env vars and restart instances).
* **Zero Data Loss:** Due to active dual-writing, Firestore has 100% of the transactions up to the failure point.

## Risks
* Incomplete sync of Firestore dual-writes could lead to minor drift (mitigated by verification tests).

## Rollback Implications
Guarantees high rollback safety during initial deployment and post-migration operations.
